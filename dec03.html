Looking back, Looking forward
 
Eighteen months ago we crossed the half-century mark, hitting our way, a la Sachin, towards the next milestone.

It has thus far been a satisfying innings. We are full of hopes and aspirations. With the usual unstinted co-operation from all Rotarians, another landmark is within our reach. The memories will certainly be cherished.

It is time to reflect, to retrace through the sands of time, on the highlights of the Rotary News since its 50thbirthday on July 15, 2002. The reflection and passage over a period of time mirrors the Rotarian-centric character of Rotary News.

There has been a soft and consistent approach towards making “Rotary News” the preferred magazine of the Indian subcontinent.

There has been a complete change in the heart of the matter, in the contents of Rotary News. In keeping with the progress of time, Rotary News has access to up-to-date technologies. Its quality too is comparable with other established news magazines. Rotary News Trust is a self-sufficient outfit now. Its in-house capabilities enables it to play an active part in communicating the ethos of Rotary, not only to the Rotarians but also to the general public.

Many innovative features have been added to the contents of Rotary News. Rotarians have contributed to the cause of spreading Rotary information through quiz and crossword puzzles, special features such as “Women in Rotary,” “Membership Development,” “Rebuilding Gujarat.” The coup de grace included a “Bulletin Contest”, “Readership Survey” which is now unveiled to gauge the Rotarian’s views on the quality and contents of Rotary News.

To come to the contributions from Rotary clubs for publication in Rotary News, emphasis should be on hands-on projects touching the lives of the community, with appropriate write-ups. Submission of monthly reports, which often happens, should be totally avoided. The photographs should depict the actual happening. Snaps showing a row of Rotarians posing for the picture do not attract other readers.

On its journey the editors may have willy-nilly trudged on a few toes, which was never intentional, but was to drive home some objective views. To put it mildly, the journey has just begun. Promises fulfilled, Rotary News will carve a niche for itself . With the support and cooperation of Rotarians, Rotary News is sure to occupy its own elitist place in the Rotary history of the Indian subcontinent.

The Dabbawallah

The famous dabbawallahas of Mumbai have time and again amazed everyone with their dexterity in switching lakhs of tiffin boxes and regrouping them again. No wonder the British monarchy thought fit to have a close encounter with them recently. Their method of operation has baffled the top academic brains in the country. A different kind of ‘dabba’ has recently made news in one district. However frivolous, the allegations of the ballot box (dabba?) being switched has brought into sharp focus the need to conduct elections in Rotary in the most transparent manner. Surely, when the RI Director has chosen to write about elections in Rotary should we not sit up and take note?

All in the Family
 
In the last couple of months some of the top brass in Rotary have expressed their anguish at the way some of our brethren in India have been conducting the Rotary affairs.

Reactions of the grass root Rotarian ranged from sorrow to agreement with the views. It would be pointing out the obvious when we say that great care needs to be exercised in admitting members into the fold of Rotary. President Jonathan has emphasized the family values of Rotary and rightly so as a means of fostering a network of friendship and fellowship.

Talking of family, a Rotarian pointed out that we have the head of the household, his wife, son, daughter-in-law, daughter and son-in-law as Rotarians. Not surprising but what is remarkable is that all of them belong to the same Rotary club. Again this did not raise eyebrows until it was revealed that the family is unwilling to admit outsiders into the Rotary clubs as members! A true example of “All in the family!”

Happy New Year
 
“Auld Lang Syne”. Let us welcome 2004, the year that will launch us into the Rotary Centennial. Wishing all the Rotarians a glorious century.
