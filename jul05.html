A NEW DAWN

Stepping into the new century with a sense of de ja vue is like celebrating birthdays. They come and go and like time and tide no one can hold it back. But I must admire RI President Carl Stenhammer?s dramatic exhortation - ?We will now write the first chapter of Rotary?s book on the second century?.

Generally speaking, history (looking back after time has elapsed) can be beneficial and at times harsh. The history of Rotary in the first one hundred years will probably be now the subject matter of many studies. We await them.

Have I got news for you

What is a good news or report is becoming increasingly difficult to define. Having been an editor of Rotary News for little over a thousand days (a sort of Sathabishekam though not in years), I am pleasantly surprised that our readers have started noticing this magazine. Atleast to say what they like and do not like. To all at this organisation of Rotary in India, it is a milestone. Admittedly, it is difficult to say what is good!

In a recent article the eminent Jurist Soli. J. Sorabjee says:- Obscenity has defied an acceptable definition because of the subjective element in it. Despite umpteen attempts,there is no internationally acceptable definition of terrorism. Definition of minority has proved a Herculean task which has eluded legal luminaries. So I derive solace whenever I come across a critical review of an article or the Rotary news magazine. In the words of T.S. Eliot:

Words strain
Crack and sometimes break
Under the burden
Under the tension, slip, slide, perish
Decay with imprecision...
Trying to learn to use words
and every attempt
Is a wholly new start
and a different kind of failure!

Create awareness and take action

Poverty and hunger are not popular issues with the political class and the third world debt is still less of an issue.For aid given by one set of contributors to be taken back in the form of interest repayments is generally seen as theoretical.The proposal to cancel all the debts of 18 of the worlds poorest countries is evidence of change. And change has happened due to strong and sustained public pressure.Popular understanding of the vicious circle of indebtedness and poverty is far superior now to what it was a generation ago. It is in rich nations interest to be perceived as profiting from the poverty of another. Laws in many countries do not allow indenturing of labour - A father cannot borrow and commit his son to pay back. It is strange that nations are allowed to and commit their future generations to onerous debts. Surely a concern to be looked into. Here is an opportunity for Rotary to enter the centre stage in poverty alleviation and to live up to the theme-Service above self.
