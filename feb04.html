Mela Time

Attending a few Rotary District Conferences certain thoughts came into mind that I share with fellow Rotarians.
 
District conferences with 8 + hours of Rotary or service thoughts is no longer the flavour of the year. Beside the opening session that seem to have an audience of 800 to 1000 + the other plenary sessions are a ritual with the invited speakers or Rotary matters addressing an audience of 80 to 100.

The ladies predictably after the opening sessions are seen outside the venue enquiring about the best possible shopping areas or direction to some entertainment shop for the kids.

You do not need a ticket to listen to Rotary discourses but you must possess one for the entertainment sessions that are packed like sardines in a tin.

Very few conferences seem to follow the clock. The clocks do not seem to wait for the politicians who invariably ‘clock’ in late.

The disappearance of issues in our midst (Rotary), which sets wide slots of time for celebrations with projection of personalities is evident throughout.

The cultural shows seem to begin and end with Indian TV and films or the fashion shows that seem to be more a display of skin than silk.

Time has come for us to decide whether Rotary District Conferences need to focus on Rotary issues at all. Seems to me that the time is for revelry, recharging of thirst and repetitive revile of “he is a jolly good fellow”.

‘Work Builds, Charity Destroys” – Baba Amte

Rajiv Mehrotra: You have been a living example of the philosophy ‘charity destroys, work builds’. From where did you conceive this idea and how did it germinate?

Baba Amte: I saw people becoming welfare addicts and I wanted to help people to help themselves. I looked at society and was confronted with deformity. I saw people with contorted bodies, others who had no thumbs, no toes and no fingers. They were unable to even show where they were in pain. Blind boys who could not see the great mountains or even a small pinch of salt on their dinner plate surrounded me. Their eyes were never caressed by rays of light, not even once in their lives. I observed children who simply sat with blank looks on their faces and I realized that they were present in this world, but totally absent in this world. Look around the globe and see how many patients are walking all alone. In Anandwan, we decided to walk together! I conceived the slogan “Charity destroys, work builds”, and as you see here, this is a world within a world, without the world. The residents produce everything themselves here, with the exception of salt, sugar and kerosene.

A mighty faith has worked miracles.

(Taken from The Mind of the Guru by eminent media personality Rajiv Mehrotra, published by Penguin Books India 2003. The book is acompilation of conversations Mr.Mehrotra had with leading personalities as part of a television series for Doordarshan.)

Mergers & Acquisitions

Every good hearted Rotarian is no doubt anxious to invite the fellowmen and women in society into Rotary world. What better service then opening the window to service. Incidentally this will also help us to reach the goal of 1.5 million (15lakhs) membership in the centennial year.

This will be a stupendous task and I have a suggestion.

Why not consider MERGERS ?

In the corporate world mergers and acquisitions are a good avenue of expansion. Why not in Rotary?

Let us invite the members of Inner Wheel to merge their organization with Rotary. It would make a lot of sense.

Most members of Inner wheel clubs are spouses of Rotarians. I believe this was once the primary condition of membership. Now that the portals of Rotary have been thrown wide open to women, and now that we have excellent presidents of Rotary clubs and distinguished Governors from this group, the reason for maintaining a separate identity does not arise. If necessary some sort of apology can be worked out for keeping them out of the fold of Rotary. If American Indian tribes demand an apology for a different cause and effect why should these ladies not ask for one?

Secondly, there are various proposals of family of Rotary including ones on membership for a family or atleast that of a Rotarian and spouse. Suggestions have also been made at club level for a reduced membership charge for them.

Thirdly, most Rotarian tends to bring their spouse to the Rotary District conferences and other Rotary functions. As members of Rotary movement their contribution can be more sufficient.

Fourthly, is the possibility of a lot more glitter and good ambience at Rotary meetings.

This can be a good beginning for the next century!
