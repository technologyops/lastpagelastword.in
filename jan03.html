Mid Winter Night's dream
With apologies to William Shakespeare
  • No Polio cases have been reported anywhere in South Asia
  • Rotary embarks on major initiatives in water management and conservation
  • Rotarians give a new thrust to vocational literacy and gainful employment for all
  • A few Rotarians have been recognised by the Nation for their outstanding work in the community
  • The Nominating Committee elects the Rotary Leaders and acrimonious elections are a thing of the past

Here is hoping that all of them come true

New Year Resolutions - inspiration from Rajaji

One event that is usually on my schedule on the 1st of January every year is the resolutions for the New Year. But after I went through an article about Rajaji, the futility of wanting more was acutely felt.

We have heard about Rajaji's intellectual prowess, his role in the freedom movement and his achievements in the political arena. But this article talked about his personal tragedies - losing his wife when he was only 37 and their youngest child at only 3, and the death of his beloved son and both sons-in-law in their prime during his lifetime. In spite of these ordeals, Rajaji maintained a calm. His brilliant intellect, steadfast conviction in his beliefs and his courage in speaking the truth at all times was what the masses saw in him - and made him a great leader to be respected and revered. However he suffered a lot of political setbacks and public humiliations; and he was neglected for a large part of his political career.

The article highlighted a Tamil verse composition penned by Rajaji, "Kurai Onrum Illai," meaning "I don't have any complaints or regrets." It is a beautiful composition in which Rajaji thanks the Almighty for all that He has blessed him, with not blaming fate or anybody for the blows life had dealt him. In fact, he is happy and thankful for all the good things that have come his way.

Isn't this an example for us to learn from this great man? Why do we keep seeking more for ourselves? Why do we blame God or others when we don't get what we want? We have to realise that we ourselves are the source of our happiness and our problems. Inner peace can be experienced only when we take things in our stride and are satisfied with whatever we are. Ultimately one should be able to say, "I'm happy," rather than "I'm successful," or "I'm wealthy."

This is my New Year resolution for 2003, thanks to Rajaji.
