NOW Rs 1000 DINNERS

While many well known politicians, industrialists, businessmen and leaders are admirers for the dislike of ostentation, I have personally seen the former Defence Minister George Fernandes travel in the economy class when in office. Rotarians appear to have taken a liking to lavish thousand-rupee dinners.

It has been reported that American Presidential contests frequently feature US $ 1000 per plate fund raisers. But the Rs 1000 dinner here will primarily benefit 5-star hotels and the beverages industry. The reason given by the organisers usually is, the occasion provides an opportunity to meet the seniormost functionaries of Rotary. Surely Rotarians are clever enough to achieve this objective by other devices!

Before I get the rejoinder - nobody is forced to attend, and in any case the Rotarians pay it out of their pocket - let me add my opinion. This may not be the best face of Rotary for the “aamadmi” ( lay public) to see.

WHERE HAVE ALL THE GRANTS GONE ?

NOW into August : Rotarians must be gearing up to submit requests for grants. But to the best of my knowledge all districts and clubs in India are currently ineligible to receive any funding from The Rotary Foundation. Why ? No Rotary District has cleared or submitted the required reports on the grants received earlier by all the clubs in their area. One more goal for the Centennial District Governors should be to clean up this mess. Clean up the augean stables in a manner of speaking.

This is one piece where I do hope that my information is incorrect!

‘COL’ AND AFTER

After every budget, people flock to experts to know their opinion on the budgetary implications.
Their interpretations only lead to confusion; as many experts, as many versions!

With the Council on Legislation (COL) just over, I feel we must have as many experts(!) in Rotary interpreting its various provisions, each Rotarian coming forward with his own version.

May be a good club service project for the Rotary Clubs!

Pulse of the club

Given the power, I would make it mandatory for every Rotary club to have a news bulletin. The periodicity of the bulletin is left to the club. But the most preferred is a weekly bulletin, as a whole lot of news could be covered without delay. A single sheet bulletin actually doesn’t cost much money except burning of midnight oil by the Editor and a few committed Rotarians. The club has a significant place in the functioning of the Rotary. An interesting aspect is that the bulletin helps in building fellowship on a personal level within the club. It also keeps members informed of the previous meetings and the day-to-day activities of the club. The bulletin can be an important tool in the club president’s hand to keep in touch with each and every member.

Circulating their bulletin to other clubs acts as a good communication of the club activities which in turn promotes goodwill amongst clubs. A few clubs have a website of their own which can be a useful communication tool in this electronic age. Does your club have a bulletin?

WHO WILL BELL THE CAT

The Council on Legislation 2004 has raised the dues payable by the Rotarians and some flap has predictably been the result. I do not believe that this alone will be a cause for any fall in numbers in our organisation.

The fact remains that RI dues constitute a small percentage (generally less than 20%) of the outlay for the privilege of the Rotary pin. What is more, every penny of this contribution is accounted for. Can the same claim sustain for the district dues, other fund raisers and the club funds. There is an urgent need to remedy the state of affairs at the club and District administration and introduce the buzz word-Transparency

Sure, the COL has stipulated some guidelines but they do not seem adequate to me. Afterall our culture is different to what is the norm in the west and the USA.

It is time that we ourselves formulated a code of conduct.
