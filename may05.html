LAST PAGE LAST WORD
 
 
by Krishnan V.Chari

Reaching for the stars

This is the time of the year of PETS in Rotary. Do not blame me, if you conjure up other images. I am referring to the training of the incoming Rotary Club leaders. Attending some of these is an interesting experience. Some focus on motivation (preaching to the converted?!) while some others seem to take on too much in terms of the scope of work in the year ahead.

In general,the sincere commitment of the incoming leaders is infectious and every one is raring to go like the horses in the starting stalls at the races. What is the rub? Are we loading them with diverse tasks rather than focusing on the key aspects of the theme and goals for the year ahead?

However, what is of paramount importance is the fact that the incoming president should be aware of the ongoing projects that the clubs are undertaking. The president should pick up the ropes and continue the projects with diligence.

Here is hoping that the leaders will digest through all the talks and discussions and make their plans and goal for the Rotary year - the first year of the second century.

Establish Credibility

It is high time that Rotary districts have their norms and conventions laid down that can be adhered to without any hassle or disputes. The Board of Directors of RI have approved the districts seeking the Board’s approval for their “incorporation” and registration. By this, all clubs in the district would be members of the incorporated district. And, the corporate documents of the district will be consistent with RI’s constitution, bylaws and policies. Indeed a blessing in disguise, with the present uncertain rules and flouted conventions not paving way for either credibility or accountability.

Where is news?

Reading the Governor’s Monthly Letters has been an enlightening experience, filling one with pride of being a Rotarian in the Centennial year. For example, DG S.R.Yogananda of RI District 3190 has documented the district’s centennial projects carried out in a striking manner. But, alas, Rotary News has not been in the loop at all, with the result many Rotarians are unaware of the great work done by Rotary in India in general. This is the reason why we have district correspondents in every district. May be, they need to be more active. Or, Rotary News has not motivated them adequately!

Peace in our lifetime

Rotary World Peace Fellowship is a Rotary Foundation Programme in which every Rotary District can participate. No contribution or allocation is expected from the District to propose a qualified candidate for the year 2006-08. I believe the reason for non-participation could be either lack of awareness or the will to pursue the task. Either way it is not too late. Clubs and Districts should work together in identifying suitable candidates and submit the application to Evanston by July 1, 2005.

While on the subject, do Rotarians appreciate that the core competence of Rotary is advocacy of peace, and the funding of the peace scholarships is from the Rotary Foundation funds which in turn represents the generosity of the Rotary family? It is critical to achieve the funding by donations from individual Rotarians or friends of Rotary.The other option is donation from the district designated fund, an option that the Rotary districts can easily avail!

The image of Rotary is created not by what we profess but by what we practice; not by what we dream, but by what we do in our clubs, in our business and professions.”

From Westerly, Bulletin of RC Madras West
