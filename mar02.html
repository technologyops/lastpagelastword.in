ARE THEY ALL ALIVE ? Krishnan V. Chari

Are they all alive? - That was the repartee of R I President Rick King, when informed by a serving Rotary Governor that the Rotary membership in his District has improved by a considerable percentage point during this year of Global Quest!

To understand this remark one needs to understand the background.

Rotary International, in their goal of increasing the membership, has promised awards (or rather rewards) to the District Governor and his/her spouse when they beat all records to increase the membership. Fair enough one would say - after all motivation is the key to success. But those people at No. ONE, Evanston did not figure on the ingenuity of the Rotarians here. So an enterprising Governor set about writing up a lot of new membership report forms, and got the tickets pronto to the R I Convention. On July 1 the membership figures dropped like a brick to the ground.

Some of the clubs were unhappy, the succeeding District Governor was in deep distress (since he had to scale higher mountains from a mirage height), and some Rotarians took it up with the higher ups.

We understand that the highest authorities in Rotary are seized of the matter of high reporting of memberships for rewards, coupled with the low reporting of membership for the payment of Rotary dues!

Now, tongue in cheek, let us look at a way to get to Barcelona Convention as guests. That is at RI expense.

The Global Quest programme requires the club to achieve a very high rate of growth. The cut off date is 24 May 2002. Remember that no RI dues are payable for Rotarians who join after 1 April 2002. The matter of subscriptions to the District or the magazine can be handled locally. So if a club shows a very high membership growth during April 2002 one stands a good chance of seeing Spain at a minimal expense. But where do we get the names? Well, a telephone directory could be one source !

To answer R I President Rick King, Yes, Sir, most of the new names should be alive, as nobody would like to keep on paying the inflated telephone bills here!



YOUTH UNDER STRESS

Rotary clubs are doing good work in schools and colleges with Interact and Rotaract clubs. However most activities revolve around careers and entertainment and RYLA’s. The Rotarians should turn their attention to counselling the students to cope with stress. Stress is mostly related to examinations and experts say that the burden of growing expectations of family and friends are making students very, very nervous. So go on Rotarians, don’t keep away from the youth thinking that they have no time for you at this time of the year. This is the time they need the soothing words most.



SELF DISCIPLINE !

At many fellowships the conversation eventually turns to the discipline of queues and obeying of rules in Singapore or the many countries of the West and how we fail on every count. Of course the culprits are not the conversationalists but the so-called illiterate masses.

Travelling to Delhi recently on the morning flight from Chennai you are in the company of elite businessman clad in suits rearing to walk in the corridors of power in Delhi for whatever is their vocation. Just imagine, as soon as flight touched down in Delhi most of these power people were up on their feet and opened the overhead lockers even before the flight was in the parking bay. Couple of bags fell on the head of the fellow flyers. The flight stewardess were pleading with all passengers to sit down and reminding them that the seat belt sign has not been switched off. Most people seemed in so much hurry and I was reminded of the adage “time is money”. May be that is the reason I was the last one to get off and go about my way!



AWARDS !

26th January besides being the Republic Day of India, also happens to be the day when the honours are handed out to many richly deserving people who have enriched our lives in one fashion or the other. The world of arts and entertainment gets the fair share with a few political figures thrown in. The share of honours for the non-governmental organisations / service organisations continues to be sparse.

The work done by Rotary in India with a truly federal organisation working at grassroots is exemplified by the Polio Plus programme. Somehow we in Rotary seem to have missed the eye of the powers to be who dole out the Padma Bhushans and the Padma Vibushans.
