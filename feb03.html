Overcrowded Stage

The editor of “The Quilon Rotarian,” Zachariah K. Samuel, makes an interesting observation in the bulletin that seems appropriate in the conference season.

“Whenever we organise a function, it is common sight that the stage is overcrowded with so many persons. Everybody considers it a prestige issue that he or she is provided a seat on the stage. To maintain the desired decorum of the meeting only a person who has a direct role in the function should be accommodated on the stage. Just compare the prize distribution ceremony of a cricket match in India and one held in some other country. In India, a minimum of 15-20 persons would occupy the stage whereas in similar functions held elsewhere the number will be 3 or 4. The whole charm of the function is spoiled by these overcrowded stages.

As a GSE team leader I attended a District Conference of 6050 in USA and at the inaugural function of the conference only three persons were on the stage - the conference chairman, the person who was to inaugurate the conference and the Rotarian who was to introduce the chief guest. The District Governor was seated in the front row. Only at the Governor’s Banquet did he occupy the stage along with his wife. Compare this to a Rotary function in this part of the country. It is a point of heated discussion who all will be seated on the stage when we organise a Rotary meeting. When a District Governor is attending a meeting, what is the relevance of the Asst. District Governor or a GGR occupying the stage? We have to accept the fact that a seat in the front rows is an honour equivalent to a seat on the stage. But, who will bell the cat?”

Food for thought indeed!

COUNTRY HAS FUNDS FOR A-BOMB
but NOT FOR HOUSES FOR POOR

I recently read a newspaper cutting that had the above banner headline. It was a remark made by a Rotarian from an European nation when handing over 16 low cost shelters in India built with the assistance of his club.

I, for one, feel sad because such remarks do not help in promoting international understanding or the Rotary cause in any way. A wag might say, “Ok, let us not build a bomb.” Then the question comes down to all kinds of armament, not just nuclear bombs.
Further the Rotarian said that his club raised these questions before the funds were released for the project: “Why do people not construct their own houses? Will the funds be utilised for constructing houses or for some other purpose?” Sitting in the welfare state of Europe, people can make statements.

Surely there is an element called dignity even if one is poor, or should we merely accept such remarks?!

Receipts against contributions to Rotary Foundation India

“Rotary Foundation (India) has been receiving contributions from Rotary Clubs and Rotarians all over India and issuing receipts in the names of more than one individual against a single cheque/demand draft as per the instructions received. The auditors of Rotary Foundation (India) have raised an objection to this practice and have pointed out that as per Indian laws and regulations it is mandatory to issue one receipt against one cheque/demand draft received and the same is to be issued to the Donor only.

In the meeting of the Executive Members of RF (I) it has been decided that with immediate effect RF(I) would issue only receipt against a single cheque/demand draft. However, the recognition may be given to more than one individual. In view of the above, it is requested that the contribution form/letter should accompany each contribution giving the details of the person like ID No., Name of the Rotary Club etc., in whose name the receipt is to be issued.”
